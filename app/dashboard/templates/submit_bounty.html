Fund an Issue

Hey there, welcome to the 'new bouny' creation flow

Are you ready to post funded work? We've seen over 300 funded issues on the Gitcoin platform; Here are some best practices:

  • Know what you're looking for - Are you looking to find a future collaborator, or just to get something done quickly? Have you outlined clear acceptance criteria for your issue? What is an acceptance time frame for your work?
  • Pick a fair price - The best rule of thumb for picking a price is to multiply (1) the number of hours it'd take a senior engineer to complete the scope * (2) the complexity of the task * (3) the hourly rate of a senior engineer. If you underfund the issue, it might not get picked up quickly (if at all).
  • Documentation is key - If it's easy for a collaborator to get up & running with your codebase; you're more likely to have success with a bounty. Does your repo enforce strong code standards and patterns, please make sure those are well-communicated!
  • Leave room for error - Especially if your task is iterative or has a qualitative acceptance criteria, it's a good practice to leave some budget for a tip at the end of the project.
  • Beware the project management triangle - Do you want work that's (1) Good, (2) Cheap, of (3) Fast? Pick 2 of the 3, and design your bounty around those constraints!
Need some inspiration? Check out Gitcoin Case Studies.

Happy Bountying!
